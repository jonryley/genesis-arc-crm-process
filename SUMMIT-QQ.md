# Summit Qualification Engine (QQ)

## The Commercial Qualification and Governance Framework

The Summit Qualification Engine (QQ) is the commercial qualification layer within the Summit Growth Engine.

It is designed to improve decision quality, governance discipline and commercial focus by ensuring opportunities progress based on evidence, not optimism.

QQ combines:
- Commercial qualification  
- Delivery qualification  
- Governance validation  
- Competitive positioning  
- Relationship assessment  
- Risk visibility  
- Commercial intelligence  

The aim is simple:

**Focus effort where organisations can genuinely win, deliver and grow.**

---

# Why QQ Exists

Most organisations waste time and resources pursuing opportunities that:
- are not real  
- are not winnable  
- are not commercially viable  
- are not deliverable  
- do not align to strategy  

Traditional CRM systems often track activity without validating opportunity quality.

The Summit Qualification Engine was designed to solve this problem.

---

# Core Qualification Principles

Every opportunity should answer four critical questions:

| Principle | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Is it Real? | Validate the opportunity genuinely exists |
| Is it Worth It? | Confirm commercial value and strategic fit |
| Can We Win It? | Assess competitive position and relationship strength |
| Can We Deliver It? | Validate operational readiness and delivery capability |

---

# Is It Real?

The opportunity must be validated before significant commercial effort is committed.

Assessment areas may include:

- Customer engagement level  
- Defined business problem  
- Funding visibility  
- Procurement clarity  
- Decision process visibility  
- Timeline credibility  
- Stakeholder access  
- Strategic alignment  

The aim is to remove false opportunities early.

---

# Is It Worth It?

Not every opportunity deserves pursuit.

Commercial qualification should assess:

- Revenue potential  
- Margin opportunity  
- Strategic account value  
- Long-term customer potential  
- Expansion potential  
- Recurring revenue opportunity  
- Market positioning value  
- Delivery complexity  
- Internal resource impact  

Summit prioritises disciplined commercial focus over pipeline volume.

---

# Can We Win It?

Qualification must assess competitive reality, not internal optimism.

Assessment areas may include:

- Existing customer relationship strength  
- Executive sponsorship  
- Differentiation  
- Competitive position  
- Customer trust  
- Past delivery credibility  
- Influence within the buying process  
- Procurement leverage  
- Strategic relevance to the client  

Strong relationships and positioning increase qualification confidence.

---

# Can We Deliver It?

Delivery readiness is assessed before major commercial commitment is made.

This is one of the core principles of Summit.

Assessment areas may include:

- Resource availability  
- Operational readiness  
- Delivery capability  
- Technical complexity  
- Partner dependencies  
- Delivery risk  
- Margin protection  
- Programme governance readiness  
- Customer delivery maturity  

Summit aligns sales and delivery from the start of the lifecycle.

---

# Qualification Scoring

QQ uses a simple weighted scoring model to support governance decisions.

Example scoring approach:

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | Weak / High Risk |
| 5 | Moderate Confidence |
| 10 | Strong Confidence |

The aim is not mathematical precision.

The aim is:
- commercial discipline  
- structured discussion  
- governance visibility  
- evidence-based progression  

---

# Qualification Outcomes

Qualification outcomes influence governance and progression decisions.

| Outcome | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Proceed | Opportunity progresses |
| Hold | More evidence required |
| Rework | Qualification gaps identified |
| Stop | Opportunity should not progress |

Fast disqualification protects strategic focus.

---

- Forecast confidence

- Pipeline health analysis  
- Deal velocity insight  
- Relationship heat mapping  
- Bid / no-bid guidance  
- Margin risk analysis

- commercial discipline

# Operating Rhythm

Qualification is not a one-time event.

Opportunities should be continuously revalidated throughout the lifecycle.

QQ supports:
- Stage progression reviews  
- Opportunity ageing reviews  
- Forecast reviews  
- Strategic pursuit reviews  
- Executive escalation  
- Delivery readiness reviews  

Qualification confidence must increase as opportunities progress.

---

- Technical stakeholder mapping  
- Political risk visibility  
- Competitor relationship analysis  
- Customer influence assessment  

Commercial success is rarely driven by process alone.

- Gates create truth

- Governance improves execution

- Commercial discipline creates scalable growth
